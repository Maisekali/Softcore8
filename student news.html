STUDENT NEWS

Students at Kenyatta University demonstrated creativity, leadership, and problem-solving skills at this year’s Annual Student Innovation Showcase, held last Friday in the university’s Innovation Hall. The event brought together undergraduates and graduate students from a wide range of academic disciplines to present their projects, research, startups, and social initiatives to faculty, peers, and local community members.

       

More than 60 projects were featured, including sustainable engineering designs, mobile apps for mental wellness, creative writing collections, and public health outreach campaigns. One of the event highlights was a group of environmental science majors who developed a low-cost, solar-powered water filtration system for rural communities—earning them the "Innovation for Impact" award.

“I loved seeing how students from different departments collaborated,” said Sarah Malik, a senior majoring in Business and Marketing. “It really shows how much talent and energy there is on campus—and how we’re encouraged to turn our ideas into something real.”

The showcase also gave students a chance to connect with alumni, industry professionals, and university partners, many of whom offered mentorship opportunities, internships, and even seed funding for promising projects. For many students, it was a stepping stone into launching their first business, publishing academic work, or contributing to real-world solutions.

       

The event is just one example of how Kenyatta University supports student innovation and celebrates student success beyond the classroom. With more events, competitions, and creative opportunities on the horizon, the future continues to look bright for the university’s next generation of changemakers.
